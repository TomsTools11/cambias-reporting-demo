1Executive Summary

Cambias Insurance Agency's LA Home campaign is spending $647/day against a $1,000/day target—a 54% gap. This analysis combines the four-layer diagnostic framework with a full right-pricing calibration against trailing 7-day RightPricing data for LA Home. Two compounding problems emerged:

1) The day-parting schedule suppresses all bids to 40–60% of their configured value during active hours and shuts off entirely 9 PM–5 AM. This is the single largest volume throttle. At the average 52% modifier, a $17.46 base bid (volume-weighted average) becomes only $9.08 effective at auction.

2) The source multipliers are severely mis-proportioned relative to market pricing. Right-pricing reveals Tier 3 (the most expensive referral source at $16.44 1st position) is set to the lowest multiplier (100%), while Tier 1 (cheapest at $8.33) is set to the highest referral multiplier (136%). The campaign is over-paying on cheap traffic and under-paying on expensive traffic, explaining Tier 3's 9.2% win rate.

The fix is a combined two-move strategy: raise the schedule to 80% (lifts all effective bids proportionally) and right-price the source multipliers (corrects the relative proportions). Together, these changes should close the volume gap to $1,000/day while actually improving blended CPA by eliminating overpay on sources like Home Search (currently 345% vs right-priced 200%).

11Methodology

This analysis combines three frameworks. The four-layer diagnostic (Layer 1: Client Context, Layer 2: Campaign Trends, Layer 3: Source Diagnosis via the Three-Metric Funnel, Layer 4: Market Calibration) identified the schedule suppression as the primary throttle and flagged ad group overlap as a structural concern. The right-pricing calibration used trailing 7-day RightPricing data for LA Home to calculate market-anchored source multipliers. Anchor ref: Tier 2 at 2nd position ($10.30)—selected as the highest-volume mid-cost source per the median-CPC method. A 15% geo-buffer was applied to account for the campaign's sub-state geographic targeting. Target position: 1st position (justified by the client's aggressive volume objective and the market's extreme concentration at 1st position, where 90–95% of clicks occur). Multipliers were calculated to produce effective bids that land between 2nd and 1st position for each source, then rounded per the rounding guidelines (nearest 5% for standard sources, nearest 10% for search channels). Anchor reference: Tier 2 @ 2nd position ($10.30). Effective bids were validated across all 8 ad groups to confirm the lowest-base ad group still clears target positions. Phased implementation recommended per the >30% change guideline. The modifier & targeting audit analyzed gender, device, property type, and carrier segmentation data (last 30 days vs last 12 months) to evaluate whether current bid modifiers and targeting exclusions are supported by performance data. The gender inversion finding (37% CPA advantage for Female vs Male) was validated against both time periods, confirming it represents a recent market shift from last year’s inverse pattern.
